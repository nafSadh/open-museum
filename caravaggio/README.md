# Caravaggio Collection

## Scope

89 works by **Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio** (1571–1610). The painter who taught European art to stage its drama in a single shaft of light — and whose violent career (he killed a man in a duel in 1606 and died four years later on a beach near Porto Ercole, aged 38) is itself the stuff of legend. The collection aims at the ~90 accepted paintings; Caravaggio left almost no drawings.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Caravaggio died on 18 July 1610 — 415 years ago.

## Collection specifics

- **One canvas per subcategory**: Commons organises Caravaggio by painting (each work has its own category with many detail shots, restoration photos, museum snapshots). The catalog keeps one representative file per painting-subcategory — e.g. "Medusa by Caravaggio" → one entry for *Medusa*, not the 38 files that Commons has. Selection prefers the shortest/cleanest filename (usually the canonical high-res scan).
- **Multiple versions**: when Caravaggio painted two versions of the same composition (*Supper at Emmaus* London + Milan; *Boy Bitten by a Lizard* London + Florence; *David with the Head of Goliath* Madrid + Vienna + Rome), both are entries, distinguished by location parenthetical in the title.
- **Subjects**: `religious` (the vast majority — altarpieces and biblical scenes), `mythological` (Medusa, Bacchus, Narcissus, Amor Vincit Omnia), `genre` (The Cardsharps, The Fortune Teller, The Musicians), `portrait`, `still_life` (just *Basket of Fruit*, the only pure still life in the output).
- **Eras**: `early` (Roman years, pre-1600), `middle` (the mature Roman commissions 1600–1606), `late` (Naples, Malta, Sicily 1606–1610 after the duel-flight).
