# Utagawa Hiroshige Collection

## Scope

363 works by **Utagawa Hiroshige** (1797–1858), the ukiyo-e printmaker famous for travel landscapes — especially the *Fifty-three Stations of the Tōkaidō* and *One Hundred Famous Views of Edo*. The collection aggregates six canonical series plus individual prints.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Hiroshige died in October 1858 — 167 years ago.

## Collection specifics

- **Series is the primary organising dimension**: all 363 entries carry a `series` field. The six scraped series:
  - *Fifty-three Stations of the Tōkaidō* (the Hōeidō 1833–34 edition)
  - *One Hundred Famous Views of Edo* (1856–1859)
  - *Kisokaidō* (sixty-nine stations, with Eisen)
  - *Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji* (Hiroshige's 1852 response to Hokusai)
  - *Famous Views of the Sixty-odd Provinces*
  - *Eight Views of Ōmi*
- **Japanese title + transliteration**: 151 of 363 have original-language titles; 102 have romanised `transliteration`. The gap is because older series on Wikipedia use English-only station names.
- **No `dimensions`**: ukiyo-e prints exist in multiple impressions; see [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md) schema policy.
- **`current_location`**: sparse (13%). Recorded where Wikipedia names a specific holding institution; most impressions circulate.
