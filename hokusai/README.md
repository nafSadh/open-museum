# Katsushika Hokusai Collection

## Scope

86 works by **Katsushika Hokusai** (1760–1849) — the Edo-period ukiyo-e master whose *Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji* (1830–1832) defined the genre's late flourish. Covers prints, paintings, and book illustrations across his seventy-year working life.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Hokusai died in May 1849 — 176 years ago.

## Collection specifics

- **Series grouping**: 72 of 86 entries carry a `series` field. Most prominently *Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji* (36 prints + 10 later additions, usually known as the "46 views"). Other series: *A Tour of the Waterfalls of the Provinces*, *Unusual Views of Famous Bridges*.
- **Japanese title**: 46 entries carry the original `japanese_title`. For the others, only the English/romanised title is recorded on Wikipedia.
- **No `dimensions`**: intentionally omitted. Ukiyo-e prints exist in many impressions across different museum collections; a single "dimension" value is misleading. Per [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md) schema policy, `dimensions` is a collection-specific field, optional here.
- **`current_location`**: partial (48 of 86); recorded for the *Hokusai Museum, Obuse* and *British Museum* holdings where Wikipedia specifies a canonical impression.
