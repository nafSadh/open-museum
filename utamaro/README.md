# Kitagawa Utamaro Collection

## Scope

367 works by **Kitagawa Utamaro** (c. 1753–1806), the ukiyo-e master whose *bijin-ga* (portraits of beautiful women) defined a sub-genre. The collection leans heavily on his half-length bijin portraits (~200 works), plus his *shunga* (erotic art) series and nature studies.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Utamaro died in 1806 — 219 years ago.

## Collection specifics

- **Subject filter**: `bijin` is by far the largest class. Other subjects include nature / bird-and-flower studies and *shunga* series (e.g. *Utamakura*).
- **No `dimensions`**: see [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md) schema policy for prints.
- **No `current_location`**: field intentionally omitted. Utamaro prints are scattered across the MFA Boston, MET, British Museum, AIC, and private collections; a single "location" value is misleading.
- **`harvest_method` field**: each entry carries a harvest-lineage tag (e.g. `wikipedia_gallery`, `wikipedia_category`, `commons_category`) recording how it was collected. This is metadata for audit/re-run purposes, not provenance — see `provenance_url` for that.
