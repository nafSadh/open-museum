# Raja Ravi Varma Collection

## Scope

241 works by **Raja Ravi Varma** (1848–1906), the Travancore-born painter who introduced Western academic oil-painting technique to Indian subjects. His depictions of scenes from the Mahabharata, Ramayana, and Puranas — reproduced as mass-market lithographs from his Bombay press — shaped the visual vocabulary of modern Hindu iconography.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Ravi Varma died in October 1906 — 119 years ago.

## Collection specifics

- **Subject grouping**: `mythology` is the largest class (Puranic / epic scenes), followed by `portrait` (royal and aristocratic commissions) and `genre` (studies of women and everyday life).
- **Sparse dates**: 38 of 241 entries still lack any date. Ravi Varma rarely dated his canvases; most dates in the literature are inferred from commission records and exhibition appearances.
- **No `dimensions`, no `current_location`**: omitted at the collection level. Many works exist as multiple lithographic editions in addition to the original paintings, and tracking a canonical "location" is ambiguous.
- **`harvest_method` field**: records harvest lineage (e.g. `wikipedia_gallery`, `wikipedia_list`, `commons_category`). Not provenance — see `provenance_url`.
