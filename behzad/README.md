# Kamāl ud-Dīn Behzād Collection

## Scope

125 works attributed to **Kamāl ud-Dīn Behzād** (c. 1450 – c. 1535), the Herat-born master miniaturist of the late Timurid and early Safavid courts. The "collection" encompasses manuscript illuminations and individual miniature pages — attribution to Behzād personally versus his workshop is debated for many of these, a caveat the catalog does not resolve.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Behzād died in the mid-1530s — nearly 500 years ago. Pre-modern. Any copyright on reproduction photographs (where it existed under sweat-of-the-brow doctrines) has long since lapsed everywhere.

## Collection specifics

- **Attribution caveat**: "by Behzād" in the catalog should be read as "attributed to Behzād or his workshop". For the canonical attributed works (about 20), consult Bahari, *Bihzad* (1996).
- **Sparse metadata**: no `dimensions`, `technique`, or `medium`. Persian miniatures are bound into manuscripts; single-sheet attribution and measurement information is not systematically captured on Wikipedia.
- **`current_location`**: recorded for 30 of 125 — primarily the Topkapı Sarayı, the British Library, and the Freer-Sackler.
- **Dates**: where recorded, dates reflect the manuscript's copying year (often from a colophon), not a separate completion year for the illumination. Treat `year_start` / `year_end` as approximate.
