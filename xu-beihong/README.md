# Xu Beihong Collection

## Scope

40 works by **Xu Beihong / 徐悲鴻** (1895–1953), the painter who shaped modern Chinese academic art by fusing Western realist technique with ink-and-wash tradition. The collection emphasises his signature horse paintings, lion and eagle studies, and a smaller set of oil-on-canvas works from his Paris period (1919–1927).

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain in life + 70 year jurisdictions** as of 2024-01-01. Xu Beihong died on 26 September 1953. China (his country of origin) uses a life + 50 rule, placing his works in PD from 2003. Life + 70 jurisdictions (EU, UK) cleared 2024; the US has followed the same threshold since the 2019 URAA restoration window lapsed. No jurisdiction currently treats his works as copyrighted.

## Collection specifics

- **Focus**: horses dominate (~half the catalog) reflecting Xu's own stated focus on the *galloping horse* motif as a symbol of Republican-era China.
- **Sparse metadata**: many entries lack `dimensions`, `current_location`, and detailed `date`. The primary source for most is Wikimedia Commons categories rather than a master catalog raisonné — no single canonical list exists in English Wikipedia.
- **Year inference**: where `date` is absent, `year_start` / `year_end` are left null rather than guessed; Xu's output spans 1910s ink studies through early-1950s commissions and the uncertainty is real.
