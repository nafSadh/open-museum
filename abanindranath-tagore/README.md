# Abanindranath Tagore Collection

## Scope

47 works by **Abanindranath Tagore** (1871–1951) — nephew of Rabindranath, founding principal of the Bengal School of Art, and the painter who consciously redirected modern Indian art away from Raja Ravi Varma's academic realism. His answer was a fluid wash-and-line technique borrowed from Mughal miniature traditions and from Japanese *nihonga* (he studied under Okakura Kakuzō's students); his subjects turned toward epic and mythological narrative, and toward nation-allegorical figures like *Bharat Mata* (1905).

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain in life + 70 year jurisdictions** since 2022. Abanindranath Tagore died on 5 December 1951. India's Copyright Act (life + 60 at his death) cleared his works in 2012; life + 70 jurisdictions in 2022. No active copyright claim exists on his paintings today.

## Collection specifics

- **Curated down from a noisy Commons crawl**. Raw category membership included photos of his house at 22 Camac Street (Kolkata), his garden residence at Konnagar, and a street named after him — all filtered out. What remains are paintings: 47 entries.
- **Signature works**: *Bharat Mata* (the four-armed Mother-India allegory), the *Arabian Nights* watercolour series, the *Krishna Lila* series, portraits of Rabindranath.
- **Eras**: `early` (pre-1905, before the *Bharat Mata* composition crystallised his nationalist mode), `middle` (1905–1920, peak Bengal School period), `late` (post-1920, more personal / mythological).
- **Sparse dates and dimensions**: Bengal School works were often undated, and Commons records for them are uneven. Where `year_start` is null, the date truly is unknown in the source.
- **Counterpoint to Ravi Varma**: keeping both collections side-by-side is deliberate — they represent opposite poles of the 1870s–1920s Indian painting debate.
