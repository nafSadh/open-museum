# Amrita Sher-Gil Collection

## Scope

169 works by **Amrita Sher-Gil** (1913–1941), the Indian-Hungarian modernist whose short career bridged European post-impressionism and Indian figurative traditions. Her paintings span three distinct periods: Paris (student works and self-portraits, 1929–1934), Hungary (portraits and village scenes, 1937–1939), and India (the mature south-Indian and village series, 1935–1941).

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain in life + 70 year jurisdictions** (EU, UK, most Commonwealth, India under current Copyright Act). Sher-Gil died on 5 December 1941. Her works entered the public domain in India in 2002 (life + 60 at the time; extended retrospectively). They have been PD worldwide under the 70-year rule since 2012.

## Collection specifics

- **Era buckets**: `paris`, `hungary`, `india` — periods of her short career.
- **Self-portraits**: numbered (e.g. *Self-Portrait 1*, *Self-Portrait 3*) because Sher-Gil painted at least nine canvases of herself between 1929 and 1935.
- **Known gaps**: 37 works have no `image_url` — mostly private-collection pieces not digitized on Commons. These carry title + dimensions + `current_location` where available.
- **Sparse metadata for older works**: a handful of early Hungary pieces lack medium/technique because primary sources (Vivan Sundaram's *Amrita Sher-Gil: A Self-Portrait in Letters and Writings*, NGMA catalog) don't record it.
