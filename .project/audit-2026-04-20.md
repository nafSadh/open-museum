# Audit — 2026-04-20

Comprehensive read-only sweep of the open-museum project against the standards stated in [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md): Wikimedia Commons image links, Wikipedia-based provenance, ISO 8601 dates, complete core metadata, and public-domain compliance.

Scope: 14 artist collections, **5,405 total artwork entries**, 14 `catalog.json` files, 14 `index.html` pages, 2 READMEs.

---

## Executive summary

| Rank | Finding | Severity | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `wikipedia_url` missing on **3,852 / 5,405 entries (71%)** — violates the AGENTS.md "provenance via Wikipedia URL at minimum" rule | **Blocker** | 13 of 14 artists (Vermeer clean) |
| 2 | Dates non-compliant with ISO 8601 on **2,981 / 5,405 entries (55%)** — 719 missing entirely, 2,262 in descriptive form (`c. 1500`, `1858–61`, `August 1882`) | **Major** | all 14 artists |
| 3 | **12 of 14 artists lack `README.md`**; only Monet and Van Gogh have one. No collection-level PD rationale for the other 12 | **Major** | 12 of 14 |
| 4 | Schema fragmentation across 3 tiers: European-complete / partial / non-Western-minimal. `utamaro` and `raja-ravi-varma` carry a `source` string ("wikipedia_gallery") which is a *tag*, not a URL — easily mistaken for provenance | **Major** | structural |
| 5 | 82 entries have empty `image_url` — Amrita Sher-Gil 38, Raja Ravi Varma 19, Utamaro 13, Monet 7, Van Gogh 3, Rembrandt 2 | Minor | 6 artists |

**Project health**: data collection is broad and correctly sourced (42/42 sampled Commons URLs resolved 200 OK), but metadata discipline lags behind the stated standards. The Western canon (Vermeer, Van Gogh, Titian, Degas) is close to compliance; the non-Western and ukiyo-e collections need a second enrichment pass for dates, provenance, and locations. No copyright-risk findings, but two borderline artists (Sher-Gil d.1941, Xu Beihong d.1953) have no documented PD basis.

## Cross-cutting findings

### 1. Provenance gap — blocker

3,852 of 5,405 entries lack a `wikipedia_url`. The worst offenders (absolute counts):

| Artist | Missing `wikipedia_url` | % of collection |
|---|--:|--:|
| monet | 1,592 | 90% |
| rembrandt | 679 | 84% |
| utamaro | 349 | 95% |
| hiroshige | 339 | 93% |
| van-gogh | 297 | 26% |
| raja-ravi-varma | 210 | 87% |
| amrita-sher-gil | 146 | 86% |
| behzad | 120 | 96% |

**Naming drift**: utamaro and raja-ravi-varma include a `source` field with the literal string `"wikipedia_gallery"` on every entry. This is a **harvest-method tag**, not a URL — easily confused with provenance. Recommend deprecating this field in favor of populating `wikipedia_url` per entry (or document `source` as an explicit non-URL tag).

### 2. Date format non-compliance — major

Per AGENTS.md: *"Dates should use ISO 8601 format (YYYY or YYYY-MM)"*. Reality:

- **719 entries** have no date at all (13% of total; concentrated in utamaro 332, ravi-varma 229, behzad 81, kuniyoshi 26, xu-beihong 25).
- **2,262 entries** have dates in non-ISO form. Representative samples:
  - van-gogh — `"November–December 1881"`, `"August 1882"`
  - vermeer — `"1654–55 \| or c. 1654–56 or c. 1655"`
  - rembrandt — `"c. 1624"`
  - titian — `"c. 1500"`, `"c. 1508"`
  - monet — `"1858–61"`
  - utamaro — `"c.\u20091793"` (U+2009 thin-space)
  - behzad — `"c. 1494–95"`

**Root cause**: art-historical dates legitimately carry uncertainty (`c.`), ranges (`1858–61`), and monthly precision (`August 1882`). Strict ISO 8601 loses information. **This is a policy issue**, not a data bug — see Policy Questions.

### 3. Schema fragmentation — major

Three tiers visible across the 14 `catalog.json` files:

| Tier | Artists | Fields present | Fields absent |
|---|---|---|---|
| A. European-complete | vermeer, titian, rembrandt, monet, van-gogh, degas | `dimensions`, `current_location`, `medium`/`technique`, `wikipedia_url` | — |
| B. Ukiyo-e partial | hokusai, hiroshige, kuniyoshi | + `japanese_title`, `series`, `transliteration` | `dimensions` (by design); `current_location` often absent |
| C. Non-Western minimal | utamaro, raja-ravi-varma, amrita-sher-gil, xu-beihong, behzad | `commons_filename`, `image_url`, `title`, `subject` | `date`, `dimensions`, `current_location`, `wikipedia_url` often missing |

Artist-specific fields vary too: `medium` (rembrandt, van-gogh, hokusai) vs `technique` (vermeer, rembrandt, hokusai, kuniyoshi) vs neither (many). Van Gogh uniquely carries `f_number`, `jh_number`, `wikidata_depicts`, `wikidata_genres`. Monet uniquely carries `w_number`, `series`, `section`.

### 4. Empty `image_url` entries — minor

82 entries have no image URL, almost entirely in three collections:

- amrita-sher-gil: **38 / 169 (22%)** — the worst offender
- raja-ravi-varma: 19 / 241 (8%)
- utamaro: 13 / 367 (4%)
- monet: 7 / 1774 (<1%)
- van-gogh: 3 / 1153
- rembrandt: 2 / 806

These should either be filled from Commons or dropped from the catalog.

### 5. Duplicate titles — informational, not a bug

| Artist | Duplicate title groups | Total duplicate entries |
|---|--:|--:|
| monet | 189 | 561 |
| van-gogh | 120 | 335 |
| rembrandt | 47 | 116 |

Spot-checking shows these are legitimate: Monet's *Haystacks*, *Water Lilies*, *Rouen Cathedral* series; Van Gogh's multiple *Sunflowers*, *Self-Portrait*; Rembrandt's repeated self-portraits. **Not spurious duplicates.** But the catalog exposes no disambiguator, so in a UI two "Haystacks" entries look identical. Consider adding a subtitle/disambiguator field (year, series index, or variant note).

### 6. `index.html` template divergence — minor

Three artists (**monet, van-gogh, titian**) use an animated `#sky` `<canvas>` element; the other 11 use a static SVG grain texture. This suggests template drift between cohorts. Not a defect, but worth noting if a consolidated template is desired later.

### 7. Documentation gaps — major (for governance)

|---|:--:|:--:|
| monet | ✓ | ✓ |
| van-gogh | ✓ | ✗ |
| others (12) | ✗ | ✗ |

Only monet and van-gogh document the collection's scope, PD rationale, and schema. The other 12 artists have identical directory structure (catalog.json, index.html, scripts/, wiki_works_raw.json) but no prose explaining the collection's boundaries or PD basis.

### Flags

- **Xu Beihong (d. 1953)**: Only 73 years since death. In 70-year-rule jurisdictions (EU, UK, most Commonwealth), his works entered PD on 2024-01-01 and are safe. In life+50 jurisdictions (Canada until 2022, Japan until 2018, China) he's been PD for decades. No jurisdiction currently treats 1953-death works as copyrighted under the life+N rule, but the margin is slim enough that an explicit README statement is advisable.
- **Amrita Sher-Gil (d. 1941)**: 85 years since death. Safe in all life+N≤70 jurisdictions. India's Copyright Act (life + 60 years) cleared her works in 2002. No risk, but the current documentation is silent.

Neither is a copyright problem *today*, but both warrant a README explicitly stating the death year, the applicable rule, and when the works entered PD — matching what Monet and Van Gogh already do.

## URL liveness spot-check

Caveat: 42 is a small sample of 5,323 non-empty image URLs (~0.8%). A comprehensive check would require a throttled full sweep (~1–2 hours) and is out of scope here. Before any large-scale re-scrape, I recommend running a full HEAD sweep as a follow-up.

## Policy questions (need human decision — not auto-fixable)

These surfaced during the audit and cannot be answered by reading code. They are decisions the project owner should make before remediation is planned.

### Q1. ISO 8601 vs art-historical date conventions

The AGENTS.md rule "ISO 8601 (YYYY or YYYY-MM)" collides with legitimate art-historical date notation: approximate dates (`c. 1500`), ranges (`1654–55`), and compound uncertainty (`c. 1654–56 or c. 1655`). Three plausible resolutions:

- **(a)** Keep `date` as a free-form display string, add structured `year_start`, `year_end`, `circa: bool` fields for filtering/sorting.
- **(b)** Force ISO (midpoint of ranges, drop `c.`), losing information.
- **(c)** Revise AGENTS.md to permit a defined superset: `YYYY`, `YYYY-MM`, `YYYY-YYYY`, `c.YYYY`, etc.

Recommendation: **(a)**. Structured siblings + free-form display gives the UI what it needs (sort by earliest year) without destroying provenance nuance.

### Q2. Mandatory `current_location` for prints?

AGENTS.md lists "current location" as a catalog field. For ukiyo-e prints and engravings, this is ill-defined — prints exist in dozens of museum collections simultaneously. Options:

- Make `current_location` required for *unique* works (paintings, drawings) and optional/absent for prints, documented in AGENTS.md.
- Add `print_edition: bool` flag so UIs can treat prints differently.

### Q3. `wikipedia_url` policy for non-Wikipedia provenance

Some works (especially from ukiyo-e collections sourced via Commons categories) don't have a dedicated Wikipedia article. Should `wikipedia_url` be:

- Always a Wikipedia *article* URL (and skip entries that don't have one), or
- Any Wikimedia URL (article, Commons page, or Commons category)?

If the latter, the field should probably be renamed to `provenance_url` to match intent.

### Q4. Deprecate the `source` field?

Utamaro and Raja Ravi Varma carry a `source: "wikipedia_gallery"` tag. It's a harvest-method label, not a source-of-truth URL. Either:

- Remove it (the harvest method belongs in script logs, not per-entry).
- Rename to `harvest_method` and keep for lineage tracking.

### Q5. Separate schema tier for non-Western collections?

Forcing AGENTS.md's Western-art-centric schema onto ukiyo-e, Persian miniatures, or Indian-calendar art may misrepresent them. Consider collection-level schema docs ("for ukiyo-e: `series` is required; `dimensions` optional; …").

## Reproducibility

All metrics in this report can be reproduced by running the following against the repo root:

```bash
python3 - <<'PY'
import json, re
from collections import Counter
artists = ['monet','vermeer','rembrandt','van-gogh','degas','hiroshige','hokusai',
           'utamaro','kuniyoshi','titian','xu-beihong','raja-ravi-varma',
           'amrita-sher-gil','behzad']
iso = re.compile(r'^\d{4}(-\d{2}(-\d{2})?)?$')
for a in artists:
    d = json.load(open(f'{a}/catalog.json'))
    n = len(d)
    wiki = sum(1 for e in d if (e.get('wikipedia_url') or '').strip())
    iso_n = sum(1 for e in d if iso.match(str(e.get('date') or '').strip()))
    img = sum(1 for e in d if (e.get('image_url') or '').strip())
    dims = sum(1 for e in d if (e.get('dimensions') or '').strip())
    loc = sum(1 for e in d if (e.get('current_location') or '').strip())
    titles = [str(e.get('title') or '').strip().lower() for e in d]
    dups = sum(1 for t,c in Counter(t for t in titles if t).items() if c>1)
    print(f"{a:<18} n={n:>5} wiki={wiki*100//n:>3}% iso={iso_n*100//n:>3}% "
          f"img={img*100//n:>3}% dims={dims*100//n:>3}% loc={loc*100//n:>3}% dup_groups={dups}")
PY
```

URL liveness uses `curl -sI -L` with a descriptive `User-Agent` header and 2.5 s spacing; Wikimedia throttles aggressive bulk HEAD traffic.
