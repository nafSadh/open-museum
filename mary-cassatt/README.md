# Mary Cassatt Collection

## Scope

~180 works by **Mary Cassatt** (1844–1926), the only American painter formally part of the French Impressionist circle. Daughter of a Pittsburgh banker who settled in Paris in the 1870s; worked alongside Degas, who became her mentor and close friend. Her signature subjects — mothers with children, women at tea, women reading — anchored a quiet, observational Impressionism that sits in productive tension with her more athletic peers.

## Copyright Status

**Public Domain Worldwide.** Cassatt died on 14 June 1926 — 99+ years ago.

## Collection specifics

- **Scale caveat**: Commons has a LOT of Cassatt, much of it redundant. The raw crawl pulled ~960 files before filtering; most of those were scans from Adelyn Breeskin's catalogue raisonné (*Mary Cassatt: A Catalogue Raisonné of the Graphic Work*, 1979) — a scholarly reference book, not artwork. Those were excluded, along with detail shots and repeat-of-the-same-painting files.
- **Types**: `painting`, `pastel`, `print`. Cassatt was a serious printmaker (especially drypoint and aquatint); prints are a substantial subset.
- **Subject filters**: `mother_child` (her signature subject), `portrait`, `print`, `genre`.
- **`current_location` / `dimensions`**: sparse — Commons extmetadata is inconsistent for her. Where a painting's Wikipedia article names a specific holding museum, it's recorded; otherwise blank.
